The structural problem

This isn't a one-time shock.
It's a compounding loop.

Each cycle ends with households weaker than the last one found them. Lost livestock, depleted savings, displacement — so when the next climate shock arrives, harvests don't just fail, they fail harder than the time before.

Pastoralist regions in Somalia have already lost 1.4 million livestock in 2025 with another 2.5 million at risk — assets that take a decade to rebuild.

Where it breaks

But cash assistance is only as good as the price signal it's calibrated against.

Today, that signal is too coarse, too backward-looking, and too aggregated to drive truly agile cash decisions in fragile contexts.

01 · Today's data

Indicators, not prices

WFP relies on composite price indicators — useful for tracking, less useful for converting into a precise cash value per household.

02 · Today's resolution

Country-level views

WFP HungerMap operates mostly at national resolution. A single country average hides huge variation between Mandera and Mombasa.

03 · Today's direction

Backward-looking

Most price tools describe what already happened. By the time a price spike registers, households have already started to feel the impact.

04 · Result

Slow, blunt cash response

Cash sizing and timing are reactive. Targeting suffers, especially in fragile regions where data is sparse to begin with.

Our solution

Forward-looking, subnational
food-price predictions —
built to translate directly into cash decisions.

01

Concrete prices,
not indicators

Forecasts the actual price of major crops (maize, sorghum, beans) — directly convertible into cash transfer values without translation layers.

02

Subnational
granularity

Predictions at Admin Level 2, enabling targeted operations county-by-county. Better targeting = higher humanitarian efficiency per dollar.

03

Flexible
time windows

Operators choose a 1–3 month forecast horizon based on the decision they're making — anticipatory action, monthly cash sizing, seasonal planning.

04

Transparent
accuracy

Region-by-region accuracy metrics shared upfront — so decision-makers know exactly when to lean on the model and when to override it.
